How To Enter Plays

The player statlines for a game will update as you enter in plays. Each play in our system can consist of 5 parts:
  • Time - The time the play took place.
  • Primary Play - The primary stat being recorded.
  • Secondary Play - The possible secondary stat being recorded.
  • Assist Play - The possible assist or potential assist that preceeded the primary play.
  • Play Rank - A description of the play if you want to highlight it.
Here are some tips on how to record plays for different situations.

Entering in Time

Try to enter in the time that the primary play occurred at. The recommended time stamp for shots is when the player is in shooting motion, but you can enter it at any moment you want really. If you are entering plays for a game that is accompanied with a video, when a play's time is clicked on the game page the video will seek to 4 seconds before that play occurred so keep that in mind.

Entering in Blocks

Plays recording blocks should be entered after the play that records field goal attempt. It's recommended to make the timestamp of a block play the same as the play that recorded the field goal attempt.

Entering in Shooting Fouls

When entering a foul that would be considered a shooting foul, don't record the shot attempt. This is how missed shooting fouls are normally recorded. If the player makes the shot during the foul then the shot should be recorded and the foul waived.
